# Design Inspiration

Hades draws on a few tools and creators we admire. This file is a living set of
references for where the app's ideas come from and where it's headed.

## Obsidian
[obsidian.md](https://obsidian.md)

The gold standard for local-first, markdown-based knowledge work. Things we take
inspiration from:

- **Local-first & plain files.** Your notes are just `.md` files on disk — no
  lock-in, sync with whatever you like. Hades' cloud sync follows the same idea.
- **`[[Wiki-links]]`** and a connected web of notes. Hades supports `[[links]]`
  and surfaces related notes as you write.
- **Live preview** that renders markdown inline while you edit, instead of a
  separate edit/preview toggle.
- **Folders + tags** for organisation, and a fast switcher / command palette
  (our ⌘K).
- A calm, keyboard-friendly, panel-based UI.

## Ulysses (and PewDiePie's workflow)
[ulysses.app](https://ulysses.app) ·
PewDiePie's ["I use this app to write"](https://www.youtube.com) videos on his
note-taking / writing setup.

Felix's enthusiasm for a **distraction-free, beautiful writing environment** is a
north star for the Notes module:

- **Typography first.** Writing should feel good — generous spacing, a clean
  serif/sans reading experience, minimal chrome.
- **One library, many sheets.** A single organised home for everything you
  write, rather than scattered files.
- **Focus on the words.** Markdown shortcuts and live formatting that get out of
  the way; export to clean PDF/markdown when you're done.
- **Goals & stats.** Writing/▸study goals and gentle progress tracking (our
  weekly goal + reflections + stats).

## North star
A study workspace that feels as calm and personal as a paper notebook, as
connected as Obsidian, and as pleasant to write in as Ulysses — with an AI
assistant that's genuinely helpful and entirely optional.

# Statistics

**What it does:** Tracks how much time you've focused, visualizes it, and measures it against a weekly goal — plus a day-streak counter and all-time totals.

**Why use it:** Seeing your focus add up is motivating, and a weekly goal turns "I should study more" into a concrete target you can hit.

It's the **Statistics** icon (bar chart) in the sidebar.

---

## What gets counted

Statistics are fed by the [Focus Timer](focus-timer.md). **Only completed *work* sessions are recorded.**

- Finish a work interval → its full duration is added to **today's** focus time, and your session count goes up.
- **Breaks don't count.**
- Resetting or switching modes **before** a work interval finishes records **nothing** for that interval.

---

## What you'll see

| Stat | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Daily / weekly / monthly / yearly focus time** | Your total focused time over each period. |
| **7-day bar chart** | A quick visual of focus time across the last week. |
| **Weekly goal + progress** | Your target hours for the week and how close you are, with motivational quotes. |
| **Day streak** | Consecutive days you've focused. |
| **All-time totals** | Everything you've logged since you started. |

---

## Set your weekly goal

**Why:** A target keeps you accountable and gives the progress bar something to fill.

1. Open **Settings** (gear icon).
2. Find **Weekly Focus Goal**.
3. Enter your target **hours per week** (default **20**, allowed range **1–168**).
4. **Save.**

Your progress toward this goal — and the motivational messaging — updates as you log focus time.

---

## Tips

- **Build a streak.** Even one short work session keeps your day streak alive — momentum matters more than volume.
- **Set a realistic goal.** A goal you hit most weeks is more motivating than an ambitious one you always miss. Adjust it anytime in Settings.

---

## Troubleshooting

**My focus time looks too low / missing.**
Only **completed work sessions** count. If you frequently pause and reset, or skip before the interval ends, that time isn't recorded. Let work intervals run to completion.

**My streak reset.**
A streak requires at least one completed work session **each day**. Miss a day and it resets — that's expected.

**Stats look wrong after changing my computer's clock or time zone.**
Daily totals are bucketed by calendar date. Large clock/time-zone changes can shift which day a session lands in.
